Tace Surpens

Attirus Canis

Part 7: Unveiling the Horror

The growl resonated through the library, growing louder with each echoing bounce off the dusty shelves. Simone and Tace followed Attirus, their hearts pounding against their ribs like trapped birds. The flashlight beam cut through the dark, revealing a labyrinth of forgotten literature and overturned furniture. They reached a heavy wooden door located at the back of the library, its surface scarred and warped with age. Attirus fumbled with a key ring, his hands shaking slightly as he unlocked the aged lock. The rusted hinges shrieked in protest as he pushed the door open, revealing a narrow, descending staircase. A wave of stale air, thick with the smell of damp earth and something far more disturbing - rotting flesh - assaulted them. Simone gagged, her hand flying to her mouth. "This way," Attirus said, his voice strained. He began to descend the steps, the beam of his flashlight flickering as he led them into the unknown. The staircase spiraled downwards, each step groaning under their weight. The air grew colder, and the stench intensified. Simone could hear Tace's shallow breaths next to her, a clear indication of his mounting fear. Finally, they reached the bottom. The cramped space opened into a large, cavernous chamber. The air here hung heavy with an oppressive darkness that the flashlight barely penetrated. Cobwebs, thick and dusty, hung from the arched ceiling, and the floor was littered with scattered bones. In the center of the chamber, bathed in the pale glow of bioluminescent mushrooms clinging to the walls, stood a horrifying sight. It was Jack, his once familiar features twisted into a grotesque parody of a human face. His eyes glowed with a malevolent red light, his body rippling with a monstrous energy. Claws protruded from his fingertips, and rows of razor-sharp teeth gleamed in a ferocious snarl. The creature that resided within Jack, the entity he had encountered in the library mirror, now had complete control. Before Simone and Tace could even let out a gasp, the creature lunged. It moved with unnatural speed, a blur of claws and teeth. Attirus shoved them both to the side, the creature's swipe tearing through the air where they had just been standing. The headmaster held his ground, raising his hand. In his grip, instead of a weapon, was a simple wooden cello bow. Simone stared in disbelief. What good was a bow against that beast? But Attirus wasn't finished. He closed his eyes and took a deep breath. Then, with a tremor in his hands, he began to play an ethereal melody. The notes resonated through the chamber, a haunting song that seemed to vibrate within their very souls. The creature froze, its feral rage replaced by confusion. It tilted its head, the red glow in its eyes flickering. The melody continued, weaving a tapestry of sorrow and regret. Tears welled up in Attirus' eyes as he played. "Jack is still in there," he shouted over the music, his voice hoarse with emotion. "Don't let it take you completely!" A flicker of recognition crossed the creature's face, a momentary glimpse of Jack struggling against the entity's control. It reached out a hand, a guttural moan escaping its throat. The melody shifted, becoming more urgent, pleading. The creature swayed back and forth, the monstrous form seeming to weaken with each note. Suddenly, the chamber filled with a deafening screech. The creature recoiled, clawing at its ears in pain. Simone realized the sound was emanating from the cello bow itself, the wood cracking and warping. With a final, agonizing shriek, the bow shattered in Attirus' hand. The creature crumpled to the floor, the red glow in its eyes fading. Jack's body lay still, unmoving. Silence descended upon the chamber, broken only by the ragged gasps of those who had just witnessed a battle fought in the depths of a man's soul. The weight of what they had just experienced pressed down on them like a physical force. Attirus slumped against the wall, his face pale and drawn. He looked at Simone and Tace, a flicker of despair in his eyes. "There's more to this story," he croaked, his voice barely a whisper. "More than you can imagine. But that comes later. Right now, we need to get Jack out of here."
