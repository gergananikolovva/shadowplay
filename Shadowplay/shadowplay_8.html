Tace Surpens

Attirus Canis

Part 8: Echoes of Humanity

The trek back to the surface was a blur. Jack's body, a lifeless husk of his former self, lay limply in Tace's arms. Simone, shaken but strangely invigorated, followed Attirus as he led them back through the labyrinth of forgotten books and broken furniture. Emerging from the library, the pale glow of dawn painted the sky with streaks of pink and orange. It felt surreal, the normalcy of the sunrise a stark contrast to the horrors they had just witnessed. Attirus led them to his car, a beat-up Volvo station wagon that looked as worn as its owner. He helped lay Jack in the back, the silence in the car broken only by the rhythmic hum of the engine. "Where are we going?" Simone finally asked, her voice hoarse. Attirus glanced at her in the rearview mirror, his eyes filled with a thousand unspoken apologies. "There's a place," he said, his voice thick with emotion. "A hidden sanctuary where they can help him. Help the entity… and maybe, just maybe, help what's left of Jack." Simone wasn't sure what to believe. After all, Attirus' involvement in the whole ordeal was shrouded in mystery. The headmaster with a cello bow battling a monster in the library? It was beyond anything she could have ever imagined. The drive felt like an eternity. They journeyed through winding backroads, leaving the familiar cityscape behind and venturing deeper into the wilderness. Finally, Attirus pulled into a clearing nestled amidst a dense forest. A weathered, two-story cabin stood nestled among the trees, smoke curling from its chimney. A woman emerged from the cabin, her long gray hair cascading down her back. Her eyes, the same shade of blue as Attirus', held a depth of wisdom that sent shivers down Simone's spine. "Attirus," the woman said, her voice a gentle whisper. "You've come." Attirus nodded, a heavy weight seeming to lift from his shoulders. He explained what had happened, his voice cracking at times. As he spoke, Simone learned Attirus harbored a dark secret. Years ago, in a car accident fueled by teenage recklessness, he had taken a life. Wracked with guilt, he dedicated himself to studying the supernatural, searching for a way to atone. His quest led him to this hidden sanctuary, a haven dedicated to containing and helping those afflicted by entities like the one that possessed Jack. The woman, his sister Lyra, was the leader of this hidden order. With a sad smile, she ushered them inside the cabin, a place that reeked of herbs and ancient knowledge. Days turned into weeks as Simone and Tace waited for news of Jack. Attirus, haggard and withdrawn, remained constantly by his side. Lyra remained tight-lipped, offering only cryptic reassurances that they were "working on it." One evening, as the fire crackled in the hearth, Lyra summoned them. Jack lay on a cot, his face pale but seemingly peaceful. His eyes fluttered open, and for the first time since they had seen him in the library, a flicker of recognition crossed his face. "Simone… Tace…" he croaked, his voice weak. Tears welled up in Simone's eyes as she rushed to his side. He looked at them, a haunted sadness lingering in his eyes. "It's not over, is it?" he whispered. Attirus, his face etched with regret, shook his head. "No, Jack. It's never truly over. But you're stronger now. You have a choice." Jack looked at each of them, a silent plea in his eyes. Then, with a shaky breath, he spoke. "Tell everyone… it was me. The murders… all of them. I was… I couldn't stop it." A gasp escaped Simone's lips. This wasn't what she expected. This wasn't a happy ending. Jack would forever take the blame for the creature's actions. But as she looked closer, she saw a flicker of resolve in his eyes. Jack had chosen to protect them, to shield them from the truth of the darkness that resided within. He would carry the burden, forever haunted by the monstrous deeds, yet still human. The journey back to Ruse was shrouded in a heavy silence. Simone and Tace carried the weight of the secret, the knowledge of a darkness lurking just beneath the surface. They knew they would never speak of the horrors they witnessed, of the hidden sanctuary, or of the creature residing within Jack. The town would mourn its victims, and Jack would be ostracized as a monster. But in a way, it was a form of justice.
